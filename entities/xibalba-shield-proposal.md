# Business Proposal: Xibalba Shield
## Bootstrapping Cryptographic HIPAA Compliance for AI Healthcare Startups
**Prepared by:** Jacob Vickers | Xibalba Solutions LLC

1. Executive Summary
The rapid adoption of agentic AI in healthcare has created a massive compliance crisis. Traditional
cybersecurity perimeters are entirely ineffective at securing decentralized, stochastic Large Language
Models (LLMs). Furthermore, the recently published Health Sector Coordinating Council (HSCC)
AI Third-Party Risk Guide (April 2026) now officially requires healthcare networks to strictly monitor
and inventory every AI tool interacting with Protected Health Information (PHI).
Xibalba Shield, powered by the Integrity Protocol, provides the solution. By leveraging smart contract
primitives (SovereignAgents and Behavioral Commitment Chains), Xibalba Shield acts as a
"Compliance-as-Code" bridge. It mathematically prevents AI agents from hallucinating or leaking PHI.
Built lean and designed for high scalability, Xibalba Shield will be bootstrapped by a solo founder
leveraging AI-accelerated development and ultra-low-cost inference APIs, completely bypassing the
need for initial venture capital.

2. The Problem & Market Catalyst
AI healthcare startups (building ambient scribes, billing bots, and conversational agents) face an
existential threat:
• The "Shadow AI" Crisis: AI tools are bypassing standard Single Sign-On (SSO) and MultiFactor Authentication (MFA) protocols. Under the new April 2026 HSCC guidelines, unmonitored
AI agents accessing Electronic Medical Records (EMRs) are considered formal HIPAA violations.
• Hallucinated Clinical Logic: A single prompt injection or context drift during a live patient
interaction can result in catastrophic data exfiltration or medically inaccurate billing codes.
• The Fragmented Compliance Trap: Startups are currently forced to pay exorbitant fees,
stringing together infrastructure platforms (like Aptible or MedStack for $20k+/year) and
compliance automation tools (like Vanta or Drata for $15k+/year), burning through their runway
just to legally operate.

3. The Solution: Xibalba Shield
Instead of forcing startups to rewrite their backends, Xibalba Shield deploys lightweight, cryptographic
guardrails targeting their most vulnerable data pipelines.
• On-Chain Agent Identity: We wrap ephemeral AI agents in unique SovereignAgent.sol
smart contracts. Every cross-silo EMR query is cryptographically bound, fully auditable, and
compliant with 2026 HSCC monitoring mandates.
• Model Contextual Integrity Protocol (MCIP): Xibalba evaluates real-time conversational
streams. If an agent attempts to deviate from authorized parameters (e.g., trying to exfiltrate raw
PHI), the transaction is instantly halted.
• Zero-Knowledge Audit Ledgers: Autonomous billing logic is wired into our AuditShield.sol
contract, allowing hospital administrators to verify the mathematical truth of AI-generated claims
without exposing raw patient charts.

4. The Bootstrapped Execution Strategy (The Solo Founder Advantage)
As a solo founder, the traditional path requires raising $1.5M in seed capital just to cover
engineering burn rates. Xibalba Shield bypasses this entirely by leveraging the 2026 AI
technology stack:
• AI-Accelerated Software Development: By utilizing advanced AI coding assistants, the
entire Integrity Protocol EVM smart contract suite and frontend dashboards can be
architected, tested, and deployed autonomously. This reduces Year 1 R&D costs to near
zero.
• Ultra-Cheap API Inference for Guardrails: Xibalba Shield will leverage ultra-cheap
inference providers. Instead of expensive dedicated clusters, background verification
checks route through providers like DeepInfra (e.g., open-source models at ~$0.08 per 1M
tokens) or Groq for instantaneous, low-latency checks.
• Account Abstraction via Paymasters: The protocol settles cryptographic proofs on
Layer-2 networks smoothly. Using ERC-4337, clients pay in fiat/stablecoins while smart
contracts operate securely in the background, achieving massive scale with zero additional
headcount.

5. The Value Proposition & Pricing Model
To undercut legacy competitors and accelerate user acquisition, Xibalba Shield utilizes a highly
competitive Base + Usage Model:
• The Offer: A startup pays a $15,000 annual base platform fee. This instantly undercuts the
friction of buying Vanta and MedStack separately.
• The Usage Upside: As the startup’s AI agents scale and pull more EMR records, they pay a
volumetric usage fee (approx. $10,000 in Year 1). We capture a margin on Paymaster gas fees
and low-cost API inference compute, ensuring revenue scales automatically with client growth.

fundamentally changes the economics of cybersecurity infrastructure. This model replaces what
